National Park Service

Re-designing the National Park Discovery & Trip Planning Tool on Mobile and Web for the National Parks across the United States

My Role

Interaction, User Research, Visual design, Design System, Prototyping & testing

Client

National Park Service

Creating the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for the visitors' digital experience

The National Park Service team wanted to improve consistency across the different park pages on their site and app. The challenge was to enable park discovery with a focus on experience, while leveraging the work and assets that they have developed for their pre-existing print design and CMS site. The MVP experience should help distribute demand between and within the sites through enhanced awareness and seamless experience. It should also encourage pre/post visit engagement to spread education and increase altruistic and volunteer participation.

Problem Statement

Visitors have limited capabilities for setting trip paramaters when looking for a park, viewing how much it cost and how to get there is challenging due to content overload and information scattered across the site. How might we help visitors search and filter information about a park, parepare for a trip, and engage them to revisit other parks.

Solution

Establish a content strategy with robust filtering to prioritize relevant information and improve navigation and discovery. Save parks and activities across the NPS Web and App including essential visit details.
